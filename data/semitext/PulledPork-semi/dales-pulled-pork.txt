Preheat oven to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C).
Season pork shoulder roast generously with sea salt and black pepper.
Heat olive oil in a 4-quart oven-safe pot with a lid over high heat.
Searseasoned pork roast in hot oil on all sides until completely browned, 2 to 3minutes per side.
Remove the pork roast to a cutting board, reserving the fatin the pot.
Allow the pot to cool slightly and place over low heat.
Cook and stir onionand garlic in reserved hot oil until the onions are translucent, about 5minutes.
Pour the chicken broth into the pot; bring to a boil while scrapingthe browned bits of food off of the bottom of the pan with a flat-edged woodenspoon.
Stir liquid smoke into the onion mixture.
Return pork roast to the potand cover with a lid.
Bake in preheated oven until completely tender, 3 to 4 hours.
Remove pork to a cutting board, cover loosely with aluminum foil, and allow itto rest for 15 minutes.
Shred the pork with a 2 forks into fine shreds.
Returnshredded pork to the pot and stir with the remaining liquid to serve.
