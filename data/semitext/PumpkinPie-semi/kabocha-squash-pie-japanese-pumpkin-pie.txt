Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
Stir the graham cracker crumbs together with the flour in a mixing bowl.
Mixin the soy butter until the mixture is crumbly.
Make a well in the middle ofthe flour mixture.
Pour in the soy milk, and stir to make a soft dough.
Turnthe dough out onto a lightly floured surface, and knead briefly.
Wrap inplastic wrap, and refrigerate for about 20 minutes.
Roll out the dough onto a lightly floured surface to about 1/4 inch thick.
Fitthe crust into a 7 inch diameter pie plate.
Use a fork to poke holes in thebottom.
Bake the crust in the preheated oven until pale gold, about 15 minutes.
Coolon a rack.
Pour about 1 inch of water into the bottom of a pan.
Place the kabocha into asteamer basket fitted into the pan.
Bring to a boil, then reduce heat tomedium, cover, and steam the squash until tender and easily pierced with afork, about 15 minutes.
Cool.
Place the kabocha into a blender or bowl of a food processer, and blend untilsmooth.
Stir the tofu, vanilla, sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg into the kabochamixture, and blend until very smooth.
Pour into the prepared crust.
Bake in preheated oven until center is set, about 20 minutes.
Do not overbakeor the filling will crack.
