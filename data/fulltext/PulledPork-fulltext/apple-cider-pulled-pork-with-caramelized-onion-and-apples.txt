apple-cider-pulled-pork-with-caramelized-onion-and-apples

Steps

Combine hard apple cider and 1/4 cup brown sugar in a slow cooker; mix until
dissolved. Add pork tenderloin.

Cook tenderloin on Low until very tender, about 6 hours. Shred pork with a
fork.

About 20 minutes for the pork is finished, melt butter in a large skillet over
medium-low heat; cook and stir onion until well browned, about 10 minutes.

Combine 1/2 cup brown sugar, cinnamon, and onion powder; season with salt and
pepper. Stir apples and sugar mixture into cooked onion. Cover and cook until
apples are tender and sauce has thickened, about 10 minutes more.

Spoon pork and apple-onion sauce over rolls to serve.

Ingredients

3 (12 fluid ounce) bottles hard apple cider

1/4 cup brown sugar

1 (2 pound) pork tenderloin

1/2 cup butter

1 large onion, cut into strips

1/2 cup brown sugar

1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

1/2 teaspoon onion powder

salt and ground black pepper to taste

2 large Granny Smith apples - peeled, cored, and sliced

6 hard rolls, split
